Short desctiption of my Tasks:

During my internship, I developed a series of high-performance data solutions focused on three critical pillars of modern data science: Scalability, 
Visualization, and Emotional Intelligence. By leveraging the Python ecosystem and industry-standard BI tools, 
I transformed millions of rows of raw data into strategic business insights.

1. Scalable Big Data Architecture (PySpark & Dask)
In this task, I addressed the challenge of "Data Gravity"—where datasets become too large for standard hardware to process. Using PySpark and Dask,
I engineered a distributed processing pipeline to analyze the NYC Taxi & Limousine Commission (TLC) dataset.

Engineering for Scalability: I utilized a Spark cluster configuration with 4GB executor memory to process over 3 million records efficiently.
Feature Engineering: I extracted temporal features such as pickup_hour and day_of_week from raw timestamps to identify peak demand periods.
Strategic Insights: My analysis revealed that while the busiest hour was 6:00 PM (214,233 trips), the highest average fare occurred at 5:00 AM ($26.90),
indicating high-value long-distance travel (e.g., airport runs) during early morning hours. I also proved that weekdays generate higher average fares ($18.95) 
compared to weekends ($18.00), despite lower volume

2. Dynamic Business Intelligence Dashboards (Power BI & Dash)
Data is only as valuable as its accessibility. I designed an interactive Sales Performance Dashboard to bridge the gap between complex backend data and
 executive decision-making.

Full-Stack Visualization: Using Python Dash (Plotly), I built a responsive web interface allowing users to filter multi-dimensional data by region and
category in real-time.

Visual Storytelling: I implemented a dual-view architecture: a Time-Series Line Chart for tracking daily sales trends and a Donut Chart for profit
contribution analysis.

Business Impact: The dashboard enables stakeholders to identify "underperforming regions" and "high-margin categories" instantly, moving from reactive 
reporting to proactive strategy.

3. Sentiment Analysis & NLP (Jupyter Notebook)
To understand the "Why" behind consumer behavior, I implemented a Sentiment Analysis pipeline using Natural Language Processing (NLP).

The NLP Pipeline: Working within a Jupyter Notebook, I performed rigorous data preprocessing, including Tokenization, Stopword removal, 
and Regex-based cleaning to remove noise from textual reviews.

Sentiment Modeling: I utilized the TextBlob Lexicon engine to calculate polarity scores, categorizing customer feedback into Positive, 
Neutral, and Negative sentiments.

Actionable Intelligence: This project provided a quantitative look at qualitative data, allowing the business to pinpoint specific 
product flaws (e.g., "broken items")or service strengths (e.g., "fast delivery") based on automated emotional detection.
